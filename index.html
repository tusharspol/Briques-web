FAQ

A few honest answers.

  1. What kind of apps can I build?

    Anything small enough that you'd build it yourself if you knew how. A weather log for fishing trips. A medication tracker. A mileage log. A workout journal. A home inventory. A water-the-plants reminder. Rule of thumb: if you currently track it on paper, in Notes, or in a spreadsheet — Briques is the right size for it.

  2. How is this different from Notion or Airtable?

    Those are tools you use to build something for a team — clients, coworkers, customers. Briques is the opposite: it builds an app for one person. You. Single-user, on your phone, no sharing, no collaboration, no logins for other people. If you've ever thought I just want this one tiny tool for me, but everything I find is built for teams — that's the gap Briques fills.

  3. What if the AI gets the app wrong?

    Tweak it in plain English ("change the date field to a dropdown"), tap regenerate, or scrap it and start over. You're never stuck with what the AI gives you on the first try. Most apps land in two or three small adjustments.

  4. Can I share my app with someone?

    No, and that's intentional. Briques is built around one rule: one app, one person. No team accounts, no shared logins, no "here's a link to my app." If you need shared, this isn't the tool. (Your data does sync between your own devices automatically.)

  5. Is my data private?

    Your apps and the data inside them are yours. We don't read them, we don't train on them, we don't sell them. AI generation runs through OpenAI and Anthropic and is governed by our Privacy Policy — give it a read if data privacy matters to you (it should).

  6. What platforms does Briques run on?

    iPhone and Android, mobile only. There's no web app — we think a phone tool deserves to actually be a phone app. iPad and other platforms aren't on the immediate roadmap.
